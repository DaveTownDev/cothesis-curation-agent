# OBS-01 — Retrospective Chart Review

**Platform code:** OBS-01  
**Taxonomy JSON internal code:** OBS-01 (codes match directly)  
**Category:** Observational Studies

---

## Definition

A retrospective observational study design that systematically extracts and analyses data from existing medical records to answer a specific research question. The researcher reviews patient charts, electronic health records, or medical databases to collect information about past events, treatments, and outcomes. The most common research methodology undertaken by medical trainees due to its accessibility, relatively low cost, and ability to be completed within training timeframes.

## When It Is Used

Use when the research question can be answered using data that already exists in medical records — no prospective data collection is required. Appropriate for describing local practice patterns, assessing treatment outcomes, exploring associations between exposures and outcomes, or generating hypotheses for prospective study.

## Key Subtypes

| Subtype | When to use |
|---|---|
| Single-site chart review | Local practice patterns, feasibility studies |
| Multi-site chart review | Generalisability, larger sample requirements |
| EMR data extraction | Large structured datasets, automated extraction |
| Hybrid chart review | Variables requiring clinical interpretation alongside structured fields |

## What Distinguishes It from Neighbouring Methods

| Compared to | Difference |
|---|---|
| Retrospective cohort study | Chart review is the data-collection method; cohort is the analytic design (comparing exposed vs unexposed groups) — these can co-occur |
| Clinical audit (EVAL-01) | Audit compares practice against explicit standards; chart review may be descriptive or hypothesis-testing without reference standards |
| Case series | Case series describes patients with a specific condition; chart review typically has broader scope and a systematic sampling frame |

## Reporting Standard

**STROBE** (Strengthening the Reporting of Observational Studies in Epidemiology) + **RECORD** (Reporting of Studies Conducted Using Observational Routinely Collected Data) for EMR-based studies.

## Quality Indicators (What a Strong Resource Looks Like)

- Inter-rater reliability reported (kappa or ICC)
- Missing data handling explicitly described
- References STROBE and/or RECORD
- Data abstraction protocol or form described
- Selection bias and information bias addressed
- Clear case definition and inclusion/exclusion criteria
