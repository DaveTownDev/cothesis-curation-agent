# SYN-01 — Narrative Systematic Review

**Platform code:** SYN-01  
**Taxonomy JSON internal code:** REV-01 (maps to SYN-01 via name; platform code is authoritative for agent output)  
**Category:** Systematic Reviews

---

## Definition

A systematic review that uses narrative synthesis rather than statistical meta-analysis to summarise findings across studies. Follows systematic search methodology but synthesises results through textual description, grouping, and tabulation. Appropriate when studies are too heterogeneous for pooling or when the research question is broad. The highest-feasibility systematic review for trainees — 49% of RANZCP scholarly projects are literature reviews, with a 93% pass rate.

## When It Is Used

Use when you need to answer a specific focused question by systematically searching and appraising the literature, but the studies are too heterogeneous (different populations, interventions, outcomes) to pool statistically. Also appropriate when the goal is qualitative integration of quantitative and qualitative evidence.

## Key Subtypes

| Subtype | When to use |
|---|---|
| Descriptive systematic review | Mapping the evidence base on a topic |
| Critical systematic review | Quality assessment is the primary focus |
| Configurative review | Exploring concepts or theory in complex interventions |

## What Distinguishes It from Neighbouring Methods

| Compared to | Difference |
|---|---|
| Meta-analysis | Narrative uses textual synthesis; meta-analysis uses statistical pooling of effect sizes |
| Scoping review (SYN-02) | Systematic review answers a specific focused question; scoping maps the breadth of evidence without quality appraisal |
| Narrative review (unsystematic) | Systematic review has explicit, reproducible search methods; narrative review may be informal |

## Reporting Standard

**PRISMA** (Preferred Reporting Items for Systematic Reviews and Meta-Analyses) + **SWiM** (Synthesis Without Meta-analysis) for the narrative synthesis methods section.

## Quality Indicators (What a Strong Resource Looks Like)

- PRISMA flow diagram present
- Registered protocol (e.g. PROSPERO)
- Quality / risk-of-bias assessment reported for included studies
- Synthesis methods described using SWiM framework
- Search strategy reproducible (databases, date range, terms)
- Explicit inclusion/exclusion criteria
