# SYN-02 — Scoping Review

**Platform code:** SYN-02  
**Taxonomy JSON internal code:** REV-02 (maps to SYN-02 via name; platform code is authoritative for agent output)  
**Category:** Systematic Reviews

---

## Definition

A type of evidence synthesis that aims to map the key concepts, types of evidence, and gaps in a research area rather than answer a specific focused question. Uses systematic search methods but with broader inclusion criteria than a systematic review. Does not require formal quality appraisal. Highly feasible for trainees and has experienced 4,200% growth in the medical education literature.

## When It Is Used

Use when the research question is exploratory or broad, the field is emerging, or you want to characterise the types and extent of evidence before deciding if a full systematic review is warranted. Also appropriate when no quality appraisal is required or feasible, and the goal is to identify research gaps rather than synthesise effect estimates.

## Key Subtypes

| Subtype | When to use |
|---|---|
| Traditional scoping review | Comprehensive literature mapping on a broad topic |
| Rapid scoping review | Abbreviated methods when time is limited |
| Evidence mapping | Visual representation of evidence to identify gaps |

## What Distinguishes It from Neighbouring Methods

| Compared to | Difference |
|---|---|
| Systematic review (SYN-01) | Systematic review answers a specific question; scoping maps the breadth of evidence — no quality appraisal required |
| Narrative review (unsystematic) | Scoping uses systematic methods; narrative review may be informal and selective |
| Rapid review | Scoping is a comprehensive mapping exercise; rapid review accelerates synthesis methods for urgency |

## Reporting Standard

**PRISMA-ScR** (PRISMA extension for Scoping Reviews). Use the PCC framework (Population, Concept, Context) for eligibility criteria.

## Quality Indicators (What a Strong Resource Looks Like)

- PRISMA-ScR compliant
- PCC framework explicitly used to define eligibility
- Charting table provided mapping included studies to key concepts
- Gap analysis section identifying areas for future research
- Search strategy reproducible across at least two databases
