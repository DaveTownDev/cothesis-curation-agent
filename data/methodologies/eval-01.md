# EVAL-01 — Standards-Based Clinical Audit

**Platform code:** EVAL-01  
**Taxonomy JSON internal code:** QI-01 (maps to EVAL-01 via name; platform code is authoritative for agent output)  
**Category:** Quality Improvement & Audit

---

## Definition

A systematic review of clinical practice against explicit evidence-based standards or guidelines. Identifies gaps between actual and desired practice — the foundation of clinical audit, comparing "what we do" against "what we should do". Highly feasible for trainees and often mandatory in UK foundation and specialty training programmes.

## When It Is Used

Use when you want to measure whether current clinical practice meets an established evidence-based standard (guideline, NICE recommendation, college policy, etc.). Appropriate for identifying quality gaps, building the case for practice change, or satisfying training requirements for audit sign-off.

## Key Subtypes

| Subtype | When to use |
|---|---|
| Structure audit | Assessing facilities, equipment, or staffing |
| Process audit | Measuring what is done to/for patients |
| Outcome audit | Measuring the results of care (mortality, complications, readmission) |

## What Distinguishes It from Neighbouring Methods

| Compared to | Difference |
|---|---|
| Research | Audit measures against pre-defined standards to improve practice; research generates new knowledge (no standard required) |
| Service evaluation | Audit uses explicit standards as the benchmark; service evaluation describes current practice without reference to a standard |
| QI project (PDSA cycle) | Audit measures the gap; QI implements the improvement intervention — audit typically precedes and follows a QI cycle |
| Chart review (OBS-01) | Chart review may be descriptive or hypothesis-testing; audit is always norm-referenced against a standard |

## Reporting Standard

**SQUIRE 2.0** (Standards for Quality Improvement Reporting Excellence). Used for reporting quality improvement initiatives including audits. Many UK college programmes also accept the Healthcare Quality Improvement Partnership (HQIP) reporting format.

## Quality Indicators (What a Strong Resource Looks Like)

- Explicit standards stated (with source/reference)
- Gap measured as a percentage or rate against the standard
- Action plan or recommendations included
- SQUIRE 2.0 compliant reporting
- Re-audit described or planned (closing the audit loop)
- Sample and denominator clearly defined
