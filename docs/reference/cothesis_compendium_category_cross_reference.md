# CoThesis Compendium — Category & Methodology Cross-Reference

**Version:** 1.0 (aligned with Registry v5.1)  
**Date:** May 2026  
**Scope:** All medical specialty trainees globally — any college, any country, any project type  
**Purpose:** Maps every Compendium entry to its CoThesis platform model ID (where one exists)

---

## How the Two Systems Relate

The **Compendium** is the user-facing resource directory. It uses categories and codes optimised for how trainees *search* — grouping by what feels natural to someone choosing a project type.

The **CoThesis platform** uses model IDs optimised for how the *architecture* works — grouping by epistemological approach, data handling, and THESIS pathway configuration.

These are deliberately different. The Compendium groups all review types under "Literature Reviews & Evidence Synthesis" (RS) because that's how trainees think. The platform separates them into SYN (Evidence Synthesis) models because they configure THESIS stages differently. Neither is wrong — they serve different purposes.

**Key structural differences:**
- The Compendium splits EVAL into two user-friendly categories: QA (Clinical Audit & Quality) and ER (Educational & Programme Research)
- The Compendium splits platform categories across multiple Compendium categories where it aids discoverability (e.g., EVAL models appear in both QA and ER; IMP models appear in HS alongside some EVAL models)
- The Compendium excludes PH3-only models (ENG, ENV) and advanced PH2 models that no medical trainee would realistically attempt (e.g., Adaptive/Platform Trials, Dose-Response Meta-Analysis)
- Three Compendium entries have no platform model at all — they exist purely for resource discoverability
- OBS-16 (Mendelian Randomisation) is excluded from the Compendium — too specialised for a trainee resource directory

## Design Decisions

| Decision | Rationale |
|----------|-----------|
| Split EVAL into QA + ER + HS | Trainees searching for "clinical audit" and "educational evaluation" have very different mental models. Three smaller categories beat one giant one for navigation. |
| Group IMP into HS (not standalone) | Implementation science as a standalone category is unfamiliar to most trainees. Placing it alongside health service evaluation matches how trainees encounter these approaches. |
| Exclude PH2/PH3 models from Compendium | A resource page with no resources is worse than no page. Models are added to the Compendium when content can back them. |
| Include IC as standalone category | Indigenous and culturally-led methodologies deserve visibility at the category level, not buried inside QUAL. Reflects CoThesis equity values. |
| Compendium-only entries (RS-18, RS-19, RS-20) | Trainees search for these. Even without platform guidance, the Compendium can point them to the right resources, books, and frameworks. |
| MV-08 as blueprint not model | Cross-cultural adaptation (Beaton framework) uses the same epistemological foundation as scale validation (MEAS-01). The Compendium still has a page for it — it just links to MEAS-01 in the platform with the CROSSCULTURAL blueprint pre-selected. |
